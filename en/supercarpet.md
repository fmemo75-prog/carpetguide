# 🏺 The Art of Carpets: From Nomadic Tents to Imperial Palaces

Every knotted rug is a physical manifestation of time and labor. To understand our collection, you must understand the "Masterpieces of Anatolia" and their ancient roots.

---

<a name="pazyryk"></a>
## ❄️ 1. The Pazyryk Carpet: The "Birth Certificate" of Rug Art
The world's oldest knotted carpet (**5th Century BC**) is the ultimate proof of our heritage.

*   **The Discovery:** Found in 1949 in the Altai Mountains of Siberia, preserved perfectly in solid ice for 2,500 years.
*   **The Technical Proof:** It features the **Ghiordes (Turkish) Double Knot**, proving that this sophisticated technique was a perfected high-art among ancient Turkic tribes millennia ago.
*   **The Motifs:** 28 horsemen and grazing stags—symbols of power and life that we still weave today.

## 👑 2. Regional Masterpieces: The Big Three

Anatolia has several weaving centers, but three names stand above the rest in global history:

### A. HEREKE: The Sultan’s Choice (The Palace Silk) 🏛️
Hereke rugs represent the pinnacle of weaving technology.
*   **Historical Prestige:** Established in 1843 as the Imperial Factory. These rugs were woven exclusively for Ottoman Palaces and world leaders.
*   **The Material:** Usually **Silk on Silk**.
*   **Technical Marvel:** A true Hereke can have **1 million to 1.5 million knots** per square meter.
*   **Aura:** Extremely fine floral patterns with a jewel-like luster.

### B. UŞAK (OUSHAK): The Star of Europe (The Aristocrat) ⭐
If Hereke is the Sultan, Uşak is the global diplomat.
*   **Renaissance Icon:** In the 15th-16th centuries, Uşak rugs appeared in the paintings of masters like **Holbein and Lotto**.
*   **Design Language:** Famous for grand scale **Star and Medallion** patterns.
*   **The Trend:** Today’s favorite for interior designers due to their soft, muted "antique-wash" pastel colors.

### C. BERGAMA: The Nomadic Soul (The Ancient Spirit) ⛰️
Bergama rugs are for those who want to touch the raw history of Anatolia.
*   **Ancient Heritage:** Carry Shamanistic and tribal motifs that have remained unchanged for centuries.
*   **The Palette:** 100% natural **Root Dyes**. Deep, saturated reds (Madder) and blues (Indigo).
*   **Style:** All-wool construction. They feel "grounded," thick, and authentic.
