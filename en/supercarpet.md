## ❄️ 1. The Pazyryk Carpet: The Birth Certificate of Rug Art

The world's oldest knotted carpet (**5th Century BC**) was preserved in solid ice for 2,500 years. This miracle of nature provides us with the ultimate proof of our weaving heritage.

*   **The Discovery:** Found in 1949 in the Altai Mountains.
*   **The Technical Proof:** It features the **Ghiordes (Turkish) Double Knot**, proving this technique was perfected by ancient Turkic tribes millennia ago.
*   **The Motifs:** 28 horsemen and grazing stags—symbols of power we still weave today.

## 👑 2. The Big Three: Regional Masterpieces

Anatolia has several weaving centers, but three names stand above the rest in global history:

### 🏛️ A. HEREKE: The Sultan’s Choice (The Palace Silk)
Hereke rugs represent the absolute pinnacle of weaving technology.
*   **Royal Heritage:** Established in 1843 as the Ottoman Imperial Factory. Woven exclusively for palaces and world leaders.
*   **Material:** Usually **Silk on Silk**, known for a jewel-like luster.
*   **Precision:** A true Hereke can have **1 million to 1.5 million knots** per square meter.

### ⭐ B. UŞAK (OUSHAK): The Star of Europe (The Aristocrat)
If Hereke is the Sultan, Uşak is the global diplomat of the rug world.
*   **Renaissance Icon:** In the 15th-16th centuries, these rugs appeared in paintings by masters like **Holbein and Vermeer**.
*   **Design:** Famous for grand scale **Star and Medallion** patterns.
*   **Modern Appeal:** Favored by top interior designers for their soft, muted **"antique-wash"** pastel colors.

### ⛰️ C. BERGAMA: The Nomadic Soul (The Ancient Spirit)
Bergama rugs are for those who want to touch the raw, unedited history of Anatolia.
*   **Ancient Heritage:** Carry Shamanistic and tribal motifs unchanged for centuries from the Pergamon region.
*   **Colors:** 100% natural **Root Dyes**. Deep, saturated Madder reds and Indigo blues.
*   **Feel:** 100% wool construction. Grounded, thick, and incredibly authentic.
