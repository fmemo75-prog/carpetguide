# 🎨 Kilim: The Ancient Geometry of the Human Soul

A Kilim is not just a rug; it is a **woven poem**. As the older ancestor of the knotted carpet, the Kilim has been the portable canvas of nomadic tribes for over 9,000 years.

## 🏛️ A Heritage Older Than History
The origins of Kilim symbols reach back to the **Neolithic era**. These designs were born from the **Shamanistic beliefs** of early Central Asian Turkic tribes, where nature and the spirit world were interconnected through the loom.

### 🏔️ Born from the Nomadic Spirit
*   **The Mountain Companion:** Kilims were lighter and easier to fold, making them perfect for high-altitude migration.
*   **The Reversible Masterpiece:** Unlike carpets, Kilims are often **reversible**, doubling the life of the art in your home.

---

## 👩‍🎨 The Silent Voice: The Dowry (Çeyiz)
For centuries, weaving was the only way for a young woman to express her emotions. Every Kilim was part of a girl's **dowry**.

<div align="center">
  <img src="./ceyiz.png" width="80%" style="border-radius: 10px; box-shadow: 0 4px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.2);" alt="The Dowry - Çeyiz">

*   **A Personal Diary:** If she was happy, her colors were bright. If she was longing for love, she used specific symbols. It was her only way to speak in a traditional world.

---

## 💃 The Iconic "Eli Belinde" (Hands on Hips)
The most important human motif in Anatolian weaving. It represents motherhood, fertility, and the "Great Mother" goddess.

<div align="center">
  <img src="./elibelinde.png" width="60%" style="border-radius: 10px; border: 2px solid #f39c12;" alt="Eli Belinde Motif">
  <p><b>The Motherhood Symbol:</b> <i>A 9,000-year-old celebration of feminine power.</i></p>
</div>

When you see this motif, you are looking at a universal symbol of creation that has survived from ancient cave paintings to modern looms.

---

## 🌿 The Alchemy of Nature: 100% Handmade
Every authentic Kilim is a product of immense patience:
1.  **Hand-Spun Wool:** Sheared from local sheep and spun by hand, creating a unique texture.
2.  **Vegetal Dyes:** Madder root for reds, Indigo for blues. These natural dyes age gracefully, gaining a beautiful "patina" over time.

---

## ⚠️ A Vanishing Art: Why Your Choice Matters
Hand-weaving is a vanishing art. When you acquire a hand-woven Kilim, you are acting as a **patron of the arts**. You are helping this ancient tradition survive for another generation.
