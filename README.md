🏺 The Art of Carpets: From Nomadic Tents to Imperial Palaces
Every knotted rug is a physical manifestation of time and labor. To understand our collection, you must understand the three "Masterpieces of Anatolia."
<a name="pazyryk"></a>
❄️ 1. The Pazyryk Carpet: The "Birth Certificate" of Rug Art
The world's oldest knotted carpet (5th Century BC) is the ultimate proof of our heritage.
The Discovery: Found in Siberia, preserved in ice for 2500 years.
The Technical Proof: It features the Ghiordes (Turkish) knot, proving that this "Double Knot" technique was not just a craft, but a perfected high-art among ancient Turkic tribes long before the dawn of modern history.
The Motifs: 28 horsemen and grazing stags—symbols of power and life that we still weave today.
<a name="types"></a>
👑 2. Regional Masterpieces: The Big Three
A. HEREKE: The Sultan’s Choice (The Palace Silk)
Hereke rugs represent the pinnacle of weaving technology.
Historical Prestige: Established in 1843 by Sultan Abdülmecid I, Hereke was the Imperial Factory. These rugs were woven exclusively for Ottoman Palaces and visiting world leaders.
The Material: Usually Silk on Silk.
Technical Marvel: They are famous for their unrivaled knot density. A true Hereke can have 1 million to 1.5 million knots per square meter.
Aura: Extremely fine floral patterns (The Seven Mountains, Flowers of the Seven Seas) with a jewel-like luster.
B. UŞAK (OUSHAK): The Star of Europe (The Aristocrat)
If Hereke is the Sultan, Uşak is the global diplomat.
Renaissance Icon: In the 15th and 16th centuries, Uşak rugs became so famous in Europe that they appeared in the paintings of Holbein, Lotto, and Vermeer.
Design Language: Known for their grand scale Star and Medallion patterns. Unlike tribal rugs, Uşaks have a more "airy" and spacious look.
The Trend: Today, they are the most sought-after rugs by interior designers worldwide due to their soft, muted, and "antique-wash" pastel colors.
C. BERGAMA: The Nomadic Soul (The Ancient Spirit)
Bergama rugs are for those who want to touch the raw, unedited history of Anatolia.
Ancient Heritage: Woven in the region of ancient Pergamon, these rugs carry Shamanistic and tribal motifs that have remained unchanged for centuries.
The Palette: 100% natural Root Dyes. Deep, saturated reds (Madder) and blues (Indigo).
Technical Style: All-wool construction. They are thicker, sturdier, and feel "grounded."
Symbolism: Every motif is a protective shield or a fertility prayer from the nomadic Yörük tribes.
🔍 Collector’s Comparison Table
Feature	👑 Hereke	⭐ Uşak	⛰️ Bergama
Material	Fine Silk / Fine Wool	Wool on Cotton	100% Wool
Patterns	Curvy Floral (Palatial)	Large Medallion (Artistic)	Geometric (Tribal)
Colors	Rich Gold, Blue, Cream	Soft Pastels, Terracotta	Deep Red, Indigo, Ochre
Investment	Museum Grade / High Value	Designer Favorite	Authentic Folk Art
💡 Expert Advice for Buyers
When choosing between these three, ask yourself:
Do you want a masterpiece of precision and luxury? Choose Hereke.
Do you want a timeless bridge between classic and modern decor? Choose Uşak.
Do you want the raw, geometric soul of the nomadic history? Choose Bergam
